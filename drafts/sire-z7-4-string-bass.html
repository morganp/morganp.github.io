Sire Z7 4-String Bass

Published: Wed 06 May 2026
By morganp

In Music.

tags: Bass Guitar Gear Review Active Electronics

The Sire Z7 is a jazz-style 4-string bass from Sire Music, a Korean brand that has built a strong reputation for delivering quality instruments at prices that undercut the competition significantly. The Z7 sits in their mid-range lineup and packs a feature set that would not look out of place on a bass costing twice as much.

Overview

The Z7 follows the classic jazz bass body shape -- offset double-cutaway alder body, maple neck, and a pair of single-coil pickups in neck and bridge positions. What sets it apart from a straightforward passive jazz bass is the onboard active preamp: Sire's own three-band EQ system with a sweepable mid frequency control.

The hardware is solid throughout. The tuners hold well, the bridge is sturdy, and the nut is cut cleanly from the factory. Setup out of the box is generally good, needing only minor truss rod and saddle tweaks to taste.

Tone Controls

The control plate holds six controls in five physical positions, arranged along a curved crescent-shaped plate:

Working left to right:

Bass -- push/pull pot that switches between active and passive modes. In active mode it controls low-frequency boost/cut. Pulling the knob up disengages the preamp entirely and runs the bass in passive mode through the Tone control only -- useful when a battery dies mid-gig or when a simpler, more organic sound is wanted.

Middle / Middle Frequency -- a stacked dual-concentric pot. The upper knob controls mid-frequency boost/cut. The lower knob sweeps the centre frequency of that mid band across a wide range, covering the fundamental body frequencies of the instrument all the way up into the upper-mid presence region. This is the most flexible control on the bass and the one that does the most to shape the character of the sound.

Treble -- high-frequency boost/cut. Works alongside the sweepable mid to dial in presence and cut without the harshness that a fixed treble shelf can sometimes introduce.

Balance -- controls the blend between the bridge and neck pickups. Full counter-clockwise selects the neck pickup alone; full clockwise selects the bridge alone; centre is both in parallel. The taper is smooth and there is no noticeable volume dip at the centre detent.

Volume / Tone -- another stacked pot on the far right. The upper knob is master volume. The lower knob is a passive tone control, which is active in both active and passive modes -- it acts as a high-frequency roll-off and is useful for taking the edge off the treble when the preamp is engaged, or as the sole tone shaping tool when running passive.

Active Preamp

The Sire preamp runs on a single 9V battery housed in a compartment on the back of the body. Battery life is reasonable -- expect several months of regular playing before needing a replacement. The preamp is relatively quiet, with no significant noise floor added over the passive signal.

The active boost/cut on each band is generous, around 15 dB each way. In practice, small amounts of cut or boost go a long way; the controls are sensitive enough that subtle adjustments make audible differences. The sweepable mid is the standout feature -- being able to find and boost or cut the exact frequency range that sits the bass in a particular mix is a significant practical advantage over fixed mid EQs.

Passive Mode

Pulling the Bass knob bypasses the preamp and connects the pickups directly to the volume and tone controls. The passive tone is warm and clear, with the characteristic jazz bass snap on the bridge pickup and a rounder, fuller sound from the neck. Some active basses lose character when switched to passive; the Z7 passive mode sounds genuinely good on its own terms.

Summary

The Sire Z7 punches well above its price bracket. The sweepable mid EQ gives real flexibility for finding a tone that works in a mix, the active/passive switching is a practical safety net, and the underlying instrument -- pickups, neck, body -- is well made. It is a capable, versatile bass whether you are recording, rehearsing, or gigging.
