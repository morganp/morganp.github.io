3D Printed Hose Clip for the ResMed AirMini Travel CPAP

Published: Wed 22 April 2026
By morganp

In Engineering.

tags: 3D Printing CPAP Travel ResMed OpenSCAD

The ResMed AirMini is an excellent travel CPAP — compact, light, and genuinely pocketable for a medical device. The one thing that frustrates me about packing it is the hose. The 13mm air hose has nowhere to go; it ends up coiled loosely in the bag, tangling with everything else.

The solution I wanted was simple: a clip that wraps around the body of the AirMini and holds the hose coiled against it, so the whole thing packs as one neat unit.

The AirMini

The AirMini measures 136 x 84 x 52mm and weighs around 300g. The air hose connects via a proprietary port on the top face of the unit.

The hose is 13mm in diameter and about 180cm long when extended. When coiled it sits at roughly 120mm diameter — just wider than the device itself.

Design Goals

  • Clip attaches and detaches without tools
  • Hose wraps around the outside of the clip, not the device
  • No permanent modification to the AirMini
  • Slim enough to not add meaningfully to pack size
  • Printable in PETG for durability
